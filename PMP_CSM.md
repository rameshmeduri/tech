#### The Agile Manitesto
---------------------------
- Written in 2001 by 17 software developers
- 12 principles and 4 unifying themes
- **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools
- **Working software** over comprehensive documentation
- **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation
- **Responding to change** over following a plan


#### Key Terms
--------------
- **Scrum** - The development strategy
- **Sprint** - One iteration of the Agile planning and executing cycle
- **Time-Box** - The length of any sprint, fixed in advance during the Scrum meeting
- **User Stories** - Short explanation in plain language by the user, describing what they do or what they need
- **Scrum Master** - aka, `The Fixer` Member of the project team responsible for moving the project forward between sprints, working with major stakeholders, and removing obstacles
- **Sprint Backlog** - The set of product items identified for the next sprint. It is a forecast of what functionality will be in the next time-box increment
- **Burndown Chart** - Shows remaining work to be done in the sprint backlog. Updated every day to show to show sprint progress
- **Product Owner** - The person representing the stakeholders and serving as the "voice of the customer." Must take an outsider view!
- **Product Backlog** - Prioritized list of everything needed in the completed project


#### during a Sprint
--------------------
- **Protect the Sprint Goal** : No changes should be made that would jeopardize or modify the established Sprint Goal. The note "Don't change horses in mid-stream" emphasizes staying the course once the work has begun.
- **Maintain Quality** : Quality goals must remain consistent and cannot be lowered to meet deadlines. The phrase "Don't get 'wobbly' on quality" serves as a reminder to uphold technical excellence throughout the process.
- **Clarify Scope** : While the goal is fixed, the specific scope can be renegotiated or clarified between the Product Owner and the development team as they uncover more information during execution.

#### PM
-----------
- PM focuses on timeline, budget, scope, and often directs the team
- SM focuses on process, coaching, removing impediments, and enabling self-organization


#### PM framework includes
- a Project Charter (scope, objectives, stakeholders), 
- a Project Plan with milestones and WBS, 
- a RAID Log, 
- weekly Status Reports, 
- Meeting Minutes (MoMs) for every key discussion
- what was delivered, test results, known issues, and next steps
